# Research backlog — "what's going on under the hood"

Threads to come back to (not needed for the first build). Activate is a
**nonprofit** (Activate Global, Inc., a 501(c)(3)), which makes a lot of this
*public* — a different, interesting model: focused on the scientist, equity-free
fellowship, non-traditional (philanthropic + government) funding rather than VC.

## 1. Activate's finances year-over-year (is the budget growing?)
- **IRS Form 990** (annual nonprofit return) — public. Total revenue, expenses,
  program spend, net assets, year over year.
  - ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer (free API + filings): search "Activate Global".
  - Cause IQ / GuideStar (Candid) for normalized history.
- Gives: budget trajectory, how much goes to fellows vs. operations, growth rate.

## 2. Who funds Activate (the donor / funder picture)
- 990 **Schedule B** lists major contributors — but names are usually redacted on
  public copies. Workaround: **reverse-lookup via funders' own 990s** —
  foundations report grants *out* (Schedule I) naming Activate as recipient. So
  query large science/climate foundations' 990s for "Activate" grants.
- **Government funders:** USAspending.gov — search Activate Global as a recipient
  of federal grants/cooperative agreements (DOE, NSF, EDA, etc.).
- Activate's own site / annual reports: "Funding Partners" page lists foundations,
  agencies, corporates publicly.
- Origin context: Activate grew out of **Cyclotron Road** at Lawrence Berkeley
  National Lab (DOE) — DOE/national-lab ties are foundational to the model.

## 3. Activate's own success metrics
- Impact/annual reports cite: 197 companies since 2015, $4B follow-on funding,
  2,800+ US jobs, new market categories. Capture their published methodology and
  track it over time; cross-check against our independent outcomes ledger
  ($58.8M NSF non-dilutive, Form D raises) for a credibility/triangulation view.

## 4. The model angle (analysis, not just data)
- Equity-free, stipend-based, non-dilutive — contrast with VC accelerators
  (YC/Techstars take equity) and with The Engine / Greentown (mixed). What does a
  philanthropy-funded, scientist-first model select for, and how do outcomes
  differ? This is the "different model, interesting" question worth a written
  analysis once the funding-history data is in.

## How this plugs into Fellow Signal
- A "Funder & Model" view: Activate's funding inflows over time (990) alongside
  the portfolio's funding *outcomes* (our ledger) — money in vs. impact out.
- Positions the whole tool as ecosystem intelligence, not just a portfolio map.
